# IMAV 2023

**3rd place, indoor challenge — the only team to complete the course fully autonomously.**
Black Bee Drones' entry for IMAV 2023 at RWTH Aachen University, Germany.

!!! warning "Legacy ROS 1 project — predates Nectar"

    This code is **ROS 1 (Noetic)** and runs on the team's first-generation SDK,
    [`tadinisdk`](https://github.com/Black-Bee-Drones/tadinisdk) — the ancestor of Nectar. It is
    **not** compatible with the current `nectar` package. It is included here for lineage and
    context, not as a Nectar usage example.

## What it did

A payload-transport task: take off, land on an ArUco-marked pickup block to attach a cone,
follow a colored rope to the drop zone, and release the cone. The published baseline run
handles the unobstructed block and the free transit route.

It is built from three composable pieces sequenced by a [SMACH](https://wiki.ros.org/smach)
state machine:

- **Follow line** — color-filter a rope, fit a line, and hold center + heading with two PID loops
  at a constant forward speed.
- **Follow ArUco** — center over a marker with two per-axis PID loops.
- **State machine** — activates each behavior in turn (search → center → land → transit → line → drop).

## Stack

- **Companion:** Raspberry Pi; **pose:** Intel RealSense T265 via `vision_to_mavros`.
- **Vision:** Raspberry Pi Camera v2 (down-facing).
- **FCU:** ArduPilot (EKF3) over MAVROS/MAVLink; servo gripper payload.
- **SDK:** `tadinisdk` (ROS 1) — control, color/ArUco detection, PID wrapper.

## Lineage to Nectar

The line/ArUco/PID patterns here were re-implemented and generalized in the ROS 2 SDK: line and
color detection now live in the [Vision module](../modules/vision/index.md), and PID control in the
[PID module](../modules/control/pid.md). The modern equivalent of the standalone ROS 1 PID node
is [`pid-controller`](https://github.com/Black-Bee-Drones/pid-controller).
