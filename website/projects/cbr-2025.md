# CBR 2025 — Flying Robot League

Black Bee Drones' autonomous system for the Flying Robot League (FRL) of the Brazilian Robotics
Competition (CBR) 2025. Four phases, all indoors without GPS, on visual odometry and computer
vision.

[CBR — Flying Robot League](https://cbr.robocup.org.br/)

## The four phases

- **Phase 1 — Localization & mapping:** boustrophedon coverage of an 8×8 m arena, YOLO landing-base
  detection (TensorRT), and a two-stage centered landing.
- **Phase 2 — Package transport:** visual-servo pickup and drop of first-aid kits with a
  servo-controlled gripper (PWM on AUX OUT 4), including orientation alignment.
- **Phase 3 — Human-robot interaction:** YOLO11n-pose gesture recognition mapping body poses to
  velocity commands, with debouncing.
- **Phase 4 — Confined-space navigation:** grid exploration of a dark maze, QR detection
  (qreader + YOLOv8), and an ESP-attached lidar over WebSocket.

Each phase is its own ROS 2 package with a Yasmin state machine.

## Stack

- **Platform:** Pixhawk 2.4.8 (ArduPilot) + Jetson Orin Nano Super.
- **Cameras:** Intel RealSense D435i (front) + IMX219 (down); lidar rangefinder for altitude.
- **Navigation:** NVIDIA Isaac ROS Visual SLAM.
- **SDK:** Nectar v0.1.0 (then named `mirela_sdk`) — control over MAVROS, the camera layer, and
  the PID controller.
- **Detection:** Ultralytics YOLOv11n and YOLOv11n-pose.
