# SAE 2026 — EletroQuad

Black Bee Drones' entry for the SAE BRASIL EletroQuad 2026 competition — the team's first
mission stack built on the **current `nectar` package** (not the earlier `mirela_sdk`).

!!! note "In progress"

    SAE 2026 is under active development and is the most complete real-world example of the
    current SDK. Full project pages will land as the repository is open-sourced; for now, the
    best reference is the deeply documented `hook` mission below.

## Missions

- **`hook` (Mission 2):** approach a sphere suspended on a hose, orient to it, lower and hook it,
  and precision-land. Documented in depth — state machine, PID parameters, simulation recipes,
  and tuning notes.
- **`faulty_or_not`:** gauge reading.
- **`bouncing`:** additional mission package.

## How it maps to Nectar

The `hook` mission uses the current `nectar` API directly — `nectar.control` for flight,
`nectar.vision` for the camera, and `nectar.ai.detection` / `nectar.ai.segmentation` for
perception. It is a faithful, end-to-end illustration of the [Get started](../get-started/index.md)
ramp applied to a full competition mission.

Source and the `hook` documentation: [Black Bee Drones organization](https://github.com/Black-Bee-Drones)
(being open-sourced).
